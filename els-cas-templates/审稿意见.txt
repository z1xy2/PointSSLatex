Dear Profressor Wang,

We have received the decision on your paper entitled "PointSS: Geometry-Aware Multi-Scale State Space Feature Learning for Point Clouds".

The Editor's and the reviewers' comments are as follows:
************************************************************


Reviewer #3: 1. The abstract is not coherent. It would be good if authors can write a sentence describing numerical results and improvement over other methods.
2. The complexity of the proposed model and the model parameter uncertainty are not enough mentioned.
3. Discussions" section should be added in a more highlighting, argumentative way. The author should analysis the reason why the tested results is achieved.
4. Authors should pattern the motivation behind using this method to explain in the introduction.
5. I suggest summarizing the related works into a table with respect to their characteristics. The authors should put their proposal into this table for easy comparison. Add more recent works such as Exploiting dynamic spatio-temporal correlations for citywide traffic flow prediction using attention based neural networks, Dynamic multi-graph spatio-temporal learning for citywide traffic flow prediction in transportation systems, An energy efficient algorithm for virtual machine allocation in cloud datacenters, Advanced computational models for urban traffic flow prediction: A comprehensive review and future directions.
6. Furthermore, the introduction section needs considerable effort (concise and brief). The problem being investigated should be described clearly, but before that, the field of research should be made clearer. Furthermore, briefly describe the major contributions in bullet form, just before the organization paragraph.
7. Do the results shown in various figures refer to a single run or multiple runs (average)? In the latter case, I will suggest adding standard deviation bars. The reason behind this is to ensure that the results overlap with the closest rivals or not.
8. Add further details on how simulations were conducted. Perhaps add a flowchart that clearly identifies how the entire system works.
9. The conclusion section also needs significant revisions. It should briefly describe the findings of the study and some more directions for further research.



Reviewer #6: This paper proposes a geometry-aware multi-scale state space framework (PointSS) to address spatial proximity loss in SSM-based point cloud processing. The idea of combining explicit geometric priors (GGAM) with adaptive scale-decoupled SSM (ASD-SSM) is meaningful and timely. The method is technically sound, and experimental results show consistent improvements over strong baselines.
However, several aspects require clarification and strengthening before publication.
* Proposes a geometry-aware multi-scale SSM framework (PointSS) to address spatial proximity loss in serialized point clouds, which is a relevant and well-motivated problem
* Combines explicit geometric priors (normals, curvature, angular relations) with adaptive state-space modeling, showing a reasonable level of technical integration
* Introduces GGAM and ASD-SSM, which together form a coherent architecture for handling local geometry and global context add relevant references,
* free automatic instance segmentation of girder bridge point cloud via large model fusion with reverse entity modelling verification
* MFINet: a multi-scale feature interaction network for point cloud registration.
* No-Reference Point Cloud Quality Assessment Through Structure Sampling and Clustering Based on Graph
* Experimental results demonstrate consistent improvements over strong baselines (e.g., PTv3, Pamba) on S3DIS and ModelNet40
* Ablation studies are relatively thorough and support the effectiveness of individual components
* Novelty is somewhat incremental; similar ideas of geometric feature enhancement and multi-scale modeling exist in prior work, and the paper should better clarify its unique contribution
* Evaluation is limited to indoor and synthetic datasets; lack of experiments on outdoor LiDAR datasets reduces generalizability
* Additional computational cost from GGAM (dual serialization, graph construction) is not sufficiently analyzed in terms of memory and runtime trade-offs
* Key hyperparameters (e.g., scale constraint factors) are manually designed and appear sensitive, with no robustness or automatic tuning analysis
* Method section, especially GGAM, is dense and could be clearer; notation is heavy and may hinder readability
* Some figures and explanations could be simplified for better accessibility
* Minor language and writing issues remain
* Recommend strengthening novelty positioning, adding broader experiments, and improving clarity before publication
* Overall assessment: technically solid with meaningful improvements, but requires moderate revision



Reviewer #8: 1. Limited Novelty
The method is largely a combination of existing techniques:
- Mamba / SSM
- Multi-scale learning
- Feature fusion
* The contribution is incremental rather than conceptual

2. Over-Complex Architecture
The framework introduces multiple modules:
- GGAM
- ASD-SSM
- Dual serialization
This raises concerns about:
Reproducibility
Practical usability

3. Weak Experimental Validation
Evaluation limited to:
- S3DIS
- ModelNet40
Missing:
Large-scale datasets (ScanNet, SemanticKITTI)
Cross-dataset validation

4. Marginal Performance Gains
Improvements over SOTA are:
Small (~0.5-1%)
Not consistently significant

5. Missing Efficiency Metrics
No:
FLOPs
Parameter count
Memory usage

6. Limited Qualitative Analysis
Visual results:
Show minor differences
Lack detailed comparison
